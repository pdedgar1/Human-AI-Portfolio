Textual Experiments

In this experiment, all four LLMs were given the same prompt: "Write a prose poem that uses slashes instead of punctuation, that approaches the experience of spring coming, a man walking the dog early in the cold morning, before anyone else is awake, and themes of quiet and embodiment!"

Experiment 1: Claude Poem

the earth holds its breath in these moments / before the thaw comes in earnest / frost still crisp beneath my boots / and the dog's eager panting clouds the air / we are the only living things stirring / except for the brave crocuses pushing through half-frozen soil / their purple heads bent like monks in prayer / my fingers ache inside wool gloves / but something in me knows the warmth is coming / like the way birds know to return / my body remembers other springs / other mornings / the quiet street belongs to us alone / each footfall echoing off empty porches / dawn barely scratching at the horizon / and my dog stops to investigate something I cannot smell / his whole being alert and present / while my mind drifts between seasons / between sleeping and waking / between the person I am in darkness and the one I become in light / the mail truck will come later / children will wait for buses / but for now the world is all potential / all breath / all beating heart and muscle and bone / moving through space like prayer
